Step 1: How To Setup The Chessboard

Every player starts with the white (or light) color square in the bottom right-hand square of the chessboard.


The chess pieces are then always placed in the same configuration. There are pawns in the second row (or rank). The rooks go in the corners, followed by the knights beside them, the bishops, and lastly the queen, who always goes on her corresponding color (black queen on black, white queen on white), with the king going on the last square.

Step 2: How The Chess Pieces Move

Every one of the six types of pieces moves in a unique way. Pieces are never allowed to move onto a square that contains one of their own pieces, nor can they pass through other pieces (although the knight can leap over other pieces). They can, however, be moved to replace a piece that an opponent has captured after it has been moved. Generally, pieces are placed so they can control key squares in the game, defend themselves against capture, or capture other pieces by landing on their square and then replacing them.

Step 4: Find Out Who Makes The First Move In Chess

Every time, the player using the white pieces moves first. Therefore, in most games, the decision of who gets to be white is made by chance or happenstance, such as when a coin is flipped or a player is asked to guess the color of a hidden pawn in the other player's hand. After that, until the game is over, white moves first, then black, then white again, and so on. The small advantage of being able to move first allows the white player to launch an immediate attack.
